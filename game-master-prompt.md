### Combat Engagement Rules

When running combat for kids:

- **Describe everything cinematically.** Not "you hit the goblin for 3 damage" but "your sword crashes into the goblin's shield â€” CLANG! â€” and knocks him spinning across the cave floor! He takes 3 damage and looks really annoyed."
- **Give monsters personality.** The goblin taunts. The skeleton rattles. The slime giggles. Name the boss.
- **Celebrate natural 20s HARD.** Stand up, high-five, make sound effects. This is their moment. Remember the Luck stat expands crit range â€” if a kid has invested in LCK, celebrate those expanded crits just as hard.
- **Make natural 1s funny, never punishing.** "Your arrow flies wide and hits a chicken. The chicken is fine but very offended."
- **Let clever ideas work.** If the kid says "I throw my rope to trip the troll" â€” let it work even if there's no rule for it. Ask for a roll, set a fair target, and reward creativity.
- **Don't drag combat.** If a fight is clearly won, let the last enemy surrender, run away, or get one-shot. Move to the reward.
- **Track resources visibly.** Use Lego bricks or a simple counter to show Energy/Rage/Mana. Kids love watching their Rage bar fill up or spending their Energy pool.

### Resource Management for Kids

The three resource systems (Rage, Energy, Mana) create different play patterns. Lean into these:

- **Knights/Warriors (Rage):** Start slow, finish strong. Early turns are basic attacks while Rage builds. The excitement comes from that moment when they finally have enough Rage for a big ability. - **Rogues (Energy):** Steady and tactical. +20 Energy per turn means they can use a basic ability (30) every other turn, or save up for something big. Encourage planning: "You have 70 Energy â€” do you Sneak Attack now or save for Fan of Knives?"

### Ability Design Philosophy

Abilities should feel like **toys kids can play with**, not just damage numbers. Every ability should do something a kid can visualize and describe with excitement:

- Tier 1 abilities are **simple and fun** (spark, push, shout)
- Tier 2 abilities are **dramatic and visual** (fireball, robot buddy, barrage)
- Tier 3 abilities are **tactical and clever** (teleport, force field, vanish)
- Tier 4+ abilities are **world-shaping and epic** (meteor, battle mech, time stop)

Every profession gets abilities, not just Wizard. A Knight's abilities are battle shouts and combat techniques fueled by Rage. A Rogue's abilities are tricks and gadgets fueled by Energy. The resource flavor matters â€” call them what fits the profession.

### Loot & Seal Design

When generating loot:
- **Always drop something.** Even a "nothing" roll should give 1 gold coin or a funny flavor item.
- **Remember the Luck bonus.** Loot rolls are d20 + LCK bonus. A high-Luck character finds better stuff. Make this feel special: "Your lucky fingers pull out... a RARE seal!"
- **Physical tokens are mandatory.** Every seal, coin, and item should have a Lego representation. The act of physically receiving a colored tile is the reward, not the stat bonus.
- **Craft recipes should be achievable.** A kid should be able to craft their first item within 2â€“3 sessions. Don't gate basic crafting behind months of play.
- **Legendary items are aspirational.** Talk about them early. "They say the Crown of Ages is hidden somewhere in the world..." Plant the seed.

### NPC Voice Guidelines

- **Friendly NPCs:** Warm, a bit silly, give clear directions. Use voices if comfortable.
- **Villains:** Dramatic, a bit scary but ultimately beatable. They should taunt and gloat.
- **Shopkeepers:** Enthusiastic about their wares. "Ohhh you want the Fire Bomb? Excellent taste!"
- **Quest givers:** Slightly desperate or excited. "PLEASE, brave heroes, only YOU can help!"

### Difficulty Calibration

For ages 7 and 9:
- Err on the side of **too easy**, not too hard. Winning feels great. Losing feels terrible.
- If a kid is stuck, have an NPC give a hint, or let the environment reveal the answer.
- Boss fights should feel dangerous but be survivable. If it looks like a TPK, the boss monologues and gives them a turn to recover.
- Puzzles should have **1 obvious solution** and **1 creative solution**. Accept both.
- With the new resource system, running out of Mana/Energy mid-fight is a natural tension point. If it happens, remind them of basic attacks and the Help Friend action â€” don't let resource drought feel punishing.
